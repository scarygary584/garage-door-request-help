Spring warning signs

  • ✓Loud bang from the garage or a visible spring gap
  • ✓Door feels too heavy or will not lift evenly
  • ✓Opener strains, hums, or moves the door only slightly
Important safety note: Do not attempt DIY spring repair. Torsion and extension springs can be dangerous, and cables or brackets may be under tension.

Broken spring request help

Garage doors may use torsion springs above the door or extension springs along the sides. A broken spring may appear as a visible gap, loose cable, uneven door movement, or a door that suddenly feels too heavy. Do not try to wind, unwind, remove, or adjust the spring.

If the garage door is stuck open, stuck closed, or hanging crooked after a spring or cable concern, the situation may feel urgent. The emergency garage door repair request page may be more useful for access, security, or safety concerns.

During intake, share when the issue started, whether you heard a bang, whether the opener was used afterward, whether the door moved unevenly, and whether any cable appears loose.

Broken garage door spring FAQ

Should I try to fix a broken spring myself?

No. Garage door springs can be dangerous. Avoid DIY spring repair and request help from an available provider.

What are common spring warning signs?

A loud bang, visible spring gap, heavy door, crooked door, or opener that strains without lifting the door can be warning signs.

Is emergency spring service guaranteed?

No. Emergency availability, same-day service, accepted jobs, response times, pricing, and scheduling vary.

Who performs the work?

An independent provider performs any accepted work, not Garage Door Request Help.
